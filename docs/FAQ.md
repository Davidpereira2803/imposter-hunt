Quick answers to common questions about The Munkeler.

## General Questions

### What is The Munkeler?

The Munkeler is a social deduction game where players try to find the Munkeler (imposter) among them. One player doesn't see the secret word â€” can you figure out who? It's perfect for parties, game nights, and travel.

### How many players do I need?

**Minimum:** 3 players  
**Maximum:** 12 players  
**Recommended:** 4-8 players for the best experience

Special roles (Jester & Sheriff) require at least 4 players.

### Do I need internet to play?

**No!** The Munkeler works completely offline. You only need internet for:
- AI topic generation
- Downloading the app initially
- Watching rewarded ads for bonus AI generations

### What platforms is it available on?

Currently available on **Android** via Google Play Store. iOS version is planned for future release.

### Is the game free?

**Yes!** The Munkeler is completely free to play with:
- No ads during gameplay
- 1 free AI generation per day
- Optional rewarded ads for 5 bonus AI generations per day

## Gameplay Questions

### How does a typical game work?

1. **Setup** - Add 3-12 players and choose a topic
2. **Roles** - Everyone sees the secret word except the Munkeler
3. **Discussion** - Players give hints about the word
4. **Voting** - Vote to eliminate the suspected Munkeler
5. **Win** - Civilians catch the Munkeler, or the Munkeler guesses the word!

See our **[How to Play](HowToPlay)** page for detailed rules and demo video.

### What are special roles?

With **4+ players**, you can enable:

- **Jester** - Tries to get voted out to win (solo victory!)
- **Sheriff** - Can inspect one player's role during the game

These add strategic depth and unexpected twists!

### How does the Munkeler win?

The Munkeler can win by:
1. **Guessing the secret word correctly** (case/accents ignored)
2. **Surviving until only 2 players remain**

### How does the Sheriff's inspection work?

The Sheriff can tap a player's card during the round phase to reveal their role (Civilian, Munkeler, or Jester). This ability can only be used **once per game**, so use it wisely!

### Can the Jester win alone?

**Yes!** If the Jester gets eliminated during voting, they win solo â€” even if civilians catch the Munkeler. This creates interesting mind games!

### What happens in a voting tie?

If votes are tied, the game proceeds to a **revote** with only the tied players. If still tied after revote, no one is eliminated that round.

### Can I play with 2 players?

The minimum is 3 players. With only 2 players, the game mechanics don't work (one Munkeler + one civilian means the Munkeler is immediately obvious).

### How many AI generations do I get?

- **Up to 5 bonus generations** by watching rewarded ads

The counter resets at midnight in your timezone.

### Are AI-generated topics saved?

**Yes!** All AI-generated topics are automatically saved locally on your device. You can access them from the topic selection screen and use them anytime, even offline.

### Can I create topics manually without AI?

**Yes!** You can create custom topics by:
1. Going to Setup screen
2. Tapping "Custom Topic"
3. Adding a name and at least 3 words (comma-separated)

No AI generations used for manual topics.

### Can I edit or delete custom topics?

Currently, custom topics are saved but cannot be edited. You can create a new version with corrections. Topic management features are planned for future updates.

## Tips & Strategy

### Any tips for playing as the Munkeler?

- Listen carefully to others' hints
- Mirror the group's confidence level
- Avoid giving specific details
- When in doubt, keep hints vague ("It's common" / "Everyone knows it")
- Make your guess when you're 70%+ confident

### How do I catch the Munkeler?

- Watch for vague or generic hints
- Notice if someone avoids direct descriptions
- Test players with follow-up questions
- Sheriff: save your inspection for when you have 2-3 suspects

### When should the Sheriff use their inspection?

**Best timing:**
- When you have 2-3 strong suspects
- Before a critical vote (to confirm/eliminate a suspect)
- Mid-game (not too early, not when obvious)

**Avoid:**
- Wasting it on random early guesses
- Saving it too late (Jester might get eliminated first)

### How do I play as the Jester?

- Act suspicious without being too obvious
- Give slightly off hints that draw attention
- Vote erratically or contradict yourself
- Make yourself a target, but don't confess outright
